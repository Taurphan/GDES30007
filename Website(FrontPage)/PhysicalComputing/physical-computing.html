Breadboards

  • A breadboard is a rectangular plastic board with tiny holes.
  • You insert electronic components (like resistors, LEDs, and wires) into these holes to prototype circuits.
  • It allows you to build and test circuits without soldering.
  • Think of it as a reusable platform for creating temporary electronic connections.

Arduinos

  • Arduinos are open-source electronics platforms.
  • They consist of a physical programmable circuit board (microcontroller) and software (IDE).
  • Arduinos read inputs (e.g., light, button presses) and turn them into outputs (e.g., activating motors, LEDs, or publishing online).

So how does THIS actually work?

A circuit is just a closed loop of electricity. When a voltage source such as a battery is connected to the circuit, it creates an electric potential difference. This causes the flow of electricity to move through the components and make things do things.

In order to close a circuit though, you need something called ground, which is essentially just the happy neutral between positive and negative. It creates a reference point, and without it the circuit wouldn't work. But we are connected to the ground all the time. So by using ourselves as the conductor and touching the live wire, the circuit just works. The code then measures this ground and makes the LED light up. Magic!
