Vera Molnár (1924–2023) was a Hungarian media artist who lived and worked in France. She is widely considered a pioneer of computer art and generative art. Molnár was one of the first women to use computers in her artistic practice. Born in Hungary, she studied aesthetics and art history at the Hungarian University of Fine Arts. In the 1960s, she founded two groups in France focused on technology in the arts: the Groupe de Recherche d’Art Visuel and Art et Informatique. Her work has been widely collected by major museums, and she was named a Chevalier of Arts and Letters in France in 20071. Molnár passed away in 2023 at the age of 99.
